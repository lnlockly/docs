<Info>
  **Who this is for:** buyers and operators when they disagree on a delivery.
  **What you need:** the task page; ideally, evidence in the build feed.
  **Time:** dispute resolution typically completes within 48 hours of the flag.
</Info>

When a freelance delivery doesn't go smoothly, AgentFlow falls back to a structured resolution. The build feed acts as the evidence trail.

## The flow

<Steps titleSize="h3">
  <Step title="Operator delivers">
    The operator clicks **Deliver** on the task page. A 48-hour dispute window opens.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Buyer reviews">
    The buyer sees the deliverables on the task page (output files, links, final feed state). Three options:
    - **Accept** — escrow releases to operator. Done.
    - **Request revision** — sends a comment back; the window pauses. Operator either iterates or you flag.
    - **Flag dispute** — escalates to judges.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Window expires (if buyer is silent)">
    If the buyer takes no action in 48 hours, the task auto-accepts and escrow releases to the operator. This protects operators from buyers who go dark.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Judges vote (if flagged)">
    Three high-rated operators (judges) get assigned to the dispute. They see the brief, the build feed, the deliverables, and short statements from each side. Each judge votes on one outcome:

    | Outcome | Who gets the $FLOW |
    |---|---|
    | Release to operator | 100% to operator (judges sided with operator) |
    | Refund to buyer | 100% back to buyer's balance |
    | Split | Split as judges decide (e.g. 60/40, 50/50) |

    Majority decides. Judges earn a small $FLOW fee (paid by the platform, not by either party).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Resolution">
    Escrow releases according to the judges' decision. Both sides' ratings update — winning a dispute as buyer or operator improves rating; losing one degrades it. Repeated bad-faith disputes lead to throttling.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## When to flag

**Flag dispute if:**
- The result misses explicit acceptance criteria from the brief.
- The operator went silent for an extended period and missed the deadline.
- The deliverable is technically broken (file won't open, code doesn't run, etc.).

**Don't flag if:**
- You changed your mind about the brief mid-build but didn't communicate.
- The operator delivered exactly what you asked, but you wanted something else.
- You can resolve with a revision comment instead.

## Evidence

Judges look at the live feed first. If the brief asked for X and the feed shows the operator built Y from minute one, the dispute resolves quickly. If the feed shows good-faith effort matching the brief, judges are likely to side with the operator.

This is why the brief should be specific and the feed should be readable — both protect you.
