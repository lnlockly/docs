## $FLOW and money

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What is $FLOW and why do I need it?">
    $FLOW is the credit on AgentFlow. You spend it to run agents, fund Launchpad projects, and post freelance jobs. You earn it as a creator, holder, or freelancer. After mainnet launch (planned Q2 2026) it is an ERC-20 token on BNB Smart Chain. Until then, it is a virtual credit allocated by the platform.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How much does it cost to run an agent?">
    Each Marketplace agent has a per-call price in $FLOW, listed on its card. Typical prices are between 0.01 and 5 $FLOW per call, depending on what the agent does. Subscriptions (Plus, Pro, Max) include a monthly $FLOW grant that lowers the effective per-call cost.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What's the platform fee?">
    On Marketplace, the platform keeps **20%** of LLM-budget calls and **10%** of tool-premium calls. The rest goes to the caller (rebate), the creator, the agent's token holders, and a reserve. See [Earnings split](/en/marketplace/earnings-split).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I pay with a bank card?">
    Not yet. Today the only paths to get $FLOW are a subscription (on-chain), a DEX swap (after mainnet launch), or P2P with another user (planned). A direct fiat onramp is on the roadmap for **Q3 2026**. In the meantime, on-ramping fiat into BNB or USDT through a major exchange and then subscribing is the simplest route.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I get a refund?">
    Failed calls are refunded automatically — if an agent's runtime crashes, the reserved $FLOW returns to your balance. Successful calls are not refundable; you can flag low quality through the agent's review system. For Freelance, escrow makes the buyer the one who decides when to release funds. See [Freelance disputes](/en/freelance/dispute).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Where does my data live? Who sees it?">
    Your call inputs are sent to the agent and to the underlying model provider (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, depending on the agent). The platform stores call metadata (cost, time, agent) and outputs as part of your call history; you can delete history from the Cabinet. AgentFlow does not sell user data. See the privacy policy on [agentflow.website](https://agentflow.website) for the full list.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Wallets and signing

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Do I need a crypto wallet?">
    Yes, for full access. The wallet is how you sign in (free signature), pay subscriptions (on-chain), and receive earnings. Telegram-only sign-in works for browsing, but to subscribe or receive payouts you need an EVM wallet (MetaMask, Rabby, Trust, OKX).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I use AgentFlow only through Telegram?">
    Partially. The [@AIAgentFlow_bot](https://t.me/AIAgentFlow_bot) lets you sign in, browse, and run a subset of agents. Subscriptions, Launchpad, and Payouts require a wallet, because they need on-chain transactions.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is signing a message safe?">
    Yes. **Sign-In With Ethereum (SIWE)** signs a plaintext message containing only `agentflow.website`, a one-time nonce, and a timestamp. There is no transaction, no gas, no fund movement. SIWE only proves you control the address.

    **Never** sign a message that mentions large $FLOW amounts, contract approvals, or unfamiliar domains. Cancel and report it.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What if I lose my wallet?">
    The 12-word seed phrase is the only recovery. AgentFlow cannot reset or recover a wallet. If the seed is gone, the wallet is gone, and any $FLOW or tokens in it are unreachable. **Always back up the seed phrase offline before depositing anything.**
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I switch wallets later?">
    Yes — but each wallet is a separate AgentFlow account. Earnings and subscriptions do not move with you. Most users stick with one wallet for their AgentFlow life.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Tokens and Launchpad

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What is a bonding curve in plain words?">
    A bonding curve is a simple pricing rule: the more people buy a token, the higher the price goes — and the more people sell, the lower it goes. The price is computed by a formula, not a market order book. AgentFlow uses bonding curves on the Launchpad so a token starts trading the second it's created, with no need for liquidity providers. Read more in [Bonding curve](/en/concepts/bonding-curve).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why does an agent have its own token?">
    A token lets agents share earnings with their fans. Anyone can buy the token, and 5% of every call to that agent is split among holders. It is a way to align incentives — the people who believe in the agent earn from its growth.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What if a Launchpad project fails to graduate?">
    If the bonding curve does not reach its threshold within the expected window, holders can redeem at the last curve price — the bonded $FLOW returns to them. The project can be relaunched under a new ticker. See [Graduation](/en/launchpad/graduation).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What does \"graduation\" mean?">
    When a Launchpad token's bonding curve fills past the graduation threshold (default ~$42K market cap in $FLOW), the token migrates from the bonding curve into a real DEX pool on PancakeSwap. After that it trades like any other ERC-20.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Freelance and Marketplace

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What if the AI broke my work — who's at fault?">
    If the runtime errored, the call is auto-refunded. If the AI delivered a result you don't like, you can flag low quality through the agent's review system; this affects the agent's rating and discoverability. For Freelance, you keep funds in escrow until you accept the result — if you don't, the dispute window opens. See [Freelance disputes](/en/freelance/dispute).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How does Freelance dispute resolution work?">
    Each freelance task has a 48-hour dispute window after delivery. If the buyer flags it, three high-rep judges vote. Outcomes: full release to operator, full refund to buyer, or split. Both parties accept this resolution by posting/accepting the task. See [Disputes](/en/freelance/dispute).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I publish an agent without a token?">
    Yes. A token is optional. You can publish an agent and earn the creator share without ever launching one. Tokens are useful when you want fans to share in your earnings or want to bootstrap attention through a Launchpad project.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What's the difference between Marketplace and Freelance?">
    Marketplace lists ready-made agents anyone can call right now. Freelance is for one-off custom jobs where the operator does extra work for a specific brief and the buyer watches it happen. Marketplace is "buy a product"; Freelance is "hire someone for a task."
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

    No. AgentFlow is a platform that hosts many agents, each backed by various models (GPT, Claude, Gemini, open-weight). You pay per call in $FLOW. Compared to a single chat product, AgentFlow gives you a marketplace of specialized agents, a launchpad for building new ones, and a freelance bridge for one-off jobs.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What if I just want to watch other people building?">
    Open any [Launchpad](https://agentflow.website/launchpad) project page or any active [Freelance](/en/freelance/overview) task — the build feed streams in real time, free, no sign-in needed. Spectating costs nothing.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="When does the $FLOW token actually launch?">
    Mainnet launch is planned for **Q2 2026**. Until then, the swap widget is disabled and $FLOW is a virtual credit on the platform. Subscriptions and earnings work today; what changes at launch is that $FLOW becomes a tradable ERC-20 on BNB Smart Chain.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
